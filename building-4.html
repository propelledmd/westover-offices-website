A Well-Positioned Asset in San Antonio's Growth Corridor

Building 4 offers 3,000 square feet of shell-only office space at the center of the Westover Offices campus, a well-positioned address within one of San Antonio's most productive employment corridors. The building's natural limestone construction and architectural standing-seam metal roof give it a professional, enduring presence that commands respect and attracts high-quality tenants or owner-operators.

Westover Hills has seen remarkable growth since this development was completed. The area now anchors San Antonio's medical and technology corridor, with major hospital systems, VA facilities, and a rapidly expanding data center ecosystem all within immediate proximity. Highway 151 delivers over 200,000 vehicles per day and connects directly to Loop 1604, making Building 4 accessible from virtually every part of the city and the surrounding metro area.

With O-1 zoning, this building is perfectly suited for medical practices, specialty clinics, law offices, financial services, insurance, tech companies, and any professional use requiring a high-quality, owned commercial address.

Ownership Structure & Responsibilities

Finish Out / Tenant Finish

Finish out is proposed by AVL Construction — the same company that built the main structures. They are specialists in tenant finish and are available to discuss each owner's needs, designs, and costs to complete the space.

Medical Surgery Ready: Building 4 is equipped with a fire suppression system, making it suitable for a medical surgery facility in addition to standard office and medical uses.

Building Owner Responsibility

  • Building exterior maintenance
  • Insurance of building
  • Owner signage
  • Property taxes on building

POA — Common Area Responsibility

  • Landscape maintenance
  • Irrigation water
  • Parking lights electricity
  • Parking lot sweeping
  • Dumpster trash collection
  • General liability insurance (common areas)
  • Property taxes on common areas
  • Management and professional fees

POA Annual Assessment

Each building owner pays a regular quarterly assessment to the Association based on an annual expense budget covering all common area maintenance and shared operating costs.
